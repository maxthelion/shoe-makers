## Lens

**Ika language** — Two completely unrelated languages share the same name "Ika": one spoken by the Arhuaco people in Colombia, the other by the Ika people in Nigeria's Delta State. The disambiguation reveals a naming collision — same label, different meanings, different contexts, no relationship.

## Connection

The shoe-makers system has its own naming collisions. The behaviour tree uses skill names like "critique", "review", "explore" that map to specific actions, but these same words appear throughout the wiki, findings, commit messages, and shift logs with varying meanings. When the explore elf reads findings that say "review the permission table," does "review" mean the adversarial review action or a general request to look at something?

More concretely, the system's `ActionType` names (`"fix-tests"`, `"critique"`, `"explore"`, etc.) serve as both machine identifiers (for tree routing) and human-readable labels (in logs, findings, prompts). This dual usage works now but creates ambiguity as the system grows — the same way "Ika" works fine within Colombia or within Nigeria but breaks at the disambiguation level.

## Proposal

Introduce a **glossary section** to the wiki that defines the system's key terms with precise, unambiguous meanings. Not just action types, but all terms that have specific system meanings: "finding", "note", "insight", "candidate", "work-item", "critique", "review", "explore", "tier", "shift", "tick", "elf".

This would live in a new wiki page (`wiki/pages/glossary.md`) and serve two purposes:
1. Help new human contributors understand the system's terminology
2. Help elves distinguish between system-specific and natural-language uses of shared terms (e.g., "review" as ActionType vs. "review" as general instruction)

## Why

As the system accumulates more wiki pages, findings, and insights, terminological ambiguity will compound. An elf reading "the reviewer should review the review findings" needs to know which "review" means what. A glossary anchors the vocabulary — it's the disambiguation page for the shoe-makers' own "Ika" collisions.
