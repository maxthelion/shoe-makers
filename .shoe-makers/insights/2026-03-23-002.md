# Insight: Provenance Chains for Elf Accountability

## Lens

**Chain of Custody** — a forensic and legal concept where the handling history of evidence must be documented at every transfer point. Each person who touches evidence must be recorded: who received it, when, from whom, and what they did with it. Breaks in the chain invalidate the evidence.

## Connection

The shoe-makers system has implicit provenance but no explicit chain. An explore elf writes `candidates.md`, a prioritise elf consumes it and writes `work-item.md`, an executor elf consumes that and writes code, a reviewer elf critiques it. But once each artifact is consumed (deleted), the chain is broken — there's no record of *which* candidate was chosen, *why* this work item was prioritised, or *which insight* led to *which implementation*.

The shift log captures action types and timestamps but not the decision graph: "candidate A was chosen over B because of reason X" or "this code was implemented because of work-item Y which came from insight Z." If a future elf finds a bug, there's no way to trace it back to the decision that caused it.

## Proposal

Add a **provenance header** to state files (`candidates.md`, `work-item.md`) and to committed artifacts (findings, insights). The header would be a simple YAML frontmatter block:

```yaml
---
parent: candidates-2026-03-23-001
chosen-from: ["candidate-1", "candidate-2", "candidate-3"]
reason: "Highest impact — closes 2 spec gaps"
created-by: prioritise
created-at: 2026-03-23T14:30:00Z
---
```

When a work item is consumed, instead of simply deleting it, append a one-line reference to the commit message: `Implements: work-item from candidates-2026-03-23-001, candidate-2`. This creates a grep-friendly provenance trail in the git history without adding new files or state.

The shift log could also include the provenance link: `- Implemented work-item (from candidate "Add reactive ratio to explore prompt", prioritised over 4 alternatives)`.

## Why

Without provenance, the system can't learn from its own decision history. If the explore elf keeps producing candidates that get dismissed, or the prioritise elf keeps picking low-impact work, there's no data to detect these patterns. A provenance chain makes the decision graph visible and enables future meta-analysis: "which types of candidates get implemented vs dismissed?" and "which insights lead to successful implementations?"

This is low-cost (just frontmatter and commit message conventions) and builds on existing patterns (the system already uses YAML frontmatter in wiki pages and skill definitions).
